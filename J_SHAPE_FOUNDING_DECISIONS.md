# PART 1 — Founding Decisions (APPROVED by Drew · 2026-06-05)

### DECISION J-1 — Capacity gates intake
**No user enters Day 111 unless an Elder slot exists.** Capacity determines intake, never
the reverse. The worst possible moment to abandon someone is the moment they finally ask for
help — so the funnel is throttled upstream rather than letting people pile up at the
threshold. Nobody is ever told "congratulations, now wait six months." If a held state is
ever unavoidable, it is honestly named and companioned, never a dead screen. *Better to
onboard slowly than to abandon at the point of asking.*

### DECISION J-2 — Start absurdly small
The founding Elder body is **Drew + 2–4 trusted founding Elders. That is all.** Not fifty,
not twenty, not ten. The first Elder Circle becomes the **template from which every future
circle is measured.** Get the DNA right first; scale only after. Capacity (J-1) will
therefore be tiny at launch — by design.

### DECISION J-3 — No minors in-app at launch
**Zero in-app J-Shape for minors at launch.** Not because minors don't need it — because
safeguarding must be *proven on adults first.* Minor risk multiplies dramatically; the adult
model must be solid before minors are ever brought in (and then only through D.A.D.D.Y. Inc.,
never the open pool).

### DECISION J-4 — Continuity of witness on re-entry
If someone leaves the J-Shape and returns, **same circle / same Elder if possible** —
because *witnessing accumulates.* A witness who already knows the story carries context that
a new one cannot. If that circle no longer exists, create a new one — but **continuity is
preferred.**

### DECISION J-5 — The Chrysalis begins inside the same relationship
**Yes — the Chrysalis begins inside the same witnessed relationship.** The Elder does not
transform the person (they cannot); the Elder witnessed the surrender. If the J-Shape is *"I
cannot do this alone,"* the Chrysalis is *"something happens when I stop trying to do it
alone."* Breaking the relationship at that exact moment would be artificial. The witness
carries forward.

# PART 2 — Counsel Review: The Elder System (operational reality)

*The question is no longer "what is the J-Shape?" but "can the human system behind it
actually exist, safely, with real and imperfect people?"*

## Council
🏛️ **Architect** (capacity & structure) · 🛡️ **Guardian** (vetting, removal, safety) ·
📖 **Teacher** (how an Elder is formed) · 🌍 **Steward** (witness≠authority, authority≠ownership) ·
🜂 **Realist** (will this survive contact with real, flawed humans?)

---

### Q1 — How many Elders are required?
- **Architect:** It is not a fixed number; it is a *governor.* Required Elders =
  (rate of people reaching the J-Shape) ÷ (J-Shapers one Elder can truly witness). With small
  async circles (~8–15 each) and capacity-gated intake (J-1), the Elder count *is* the
  throttle on the whole system.
- **Realist:** And throughput is lower than it looks — a person *hangs* in the J-Shape for a
  variable, possibly long time, so slots turn over slowly. Plan for low flow. At launch with
  3–5 Elders (J-2), capacity is intentionally tiny. **Good.** The human is the scarce,
  precious resource, and treating it as the constraint is correct, not a failure.
- **Verdict:** Elders are the deliberate bottleneck. Self-scaling later (graduates → Elders)
  grows capacity organically as the base matures.

### Q2 — How are Elders selected?
- **Steward:** By the Gardener principle (Constitution Art. VII): *the one who chases the role
  is disqualified by the chasing.* Elders are **recognized and invited, never self-nominated
  by application.** Volunteers eager to be witnesses/authorities are a red flag.
- **Guardian:** Layered on top of recognition: hard safety vetting — background check,
  references, identity. Recognition selects for character; vetting screens for danger. Both
  required; neither sufficient alone.
- **Teacher:** Drawn from those who **came through the J-Shape themselves** and then showed,
  by pattern, that they tend without needing recognition. You select the Elder by the same
  evidence the Ark trusts everywhere: a witnessed pattern of service, not a résumé.
- **Verdict:** Invited (not applied-for) + safety-vetted + chosen for demonstrated tending.

### Q3 — How are Elders trained?
- **Teacher:** The core skill is **witnessing without fixing** — and it runs against every
  helper's instinct. Training must *un-teach the savior reflex* (Day 105: you cannot save
  them). An Elder who rushes to rescue will short-circuit the surrender the J-Shape exists to
  allow.
- **Realist:** A course won't do it. Training is **apprenticeship** — a new Elder sits inside
  a circle held by a seasoned one before holding their own. (This is *why* J-2's founding
  circle DNA matters: every future Elder is descended from it.)
- **Guardian:** Plus non-negotiable protocol training: safeguarding, mandated reporting,
  two-deep, escalation, privacy (witness-note not transcript), when to flag.
- **Verdict:** Relational apprenticeship + protocol certification. Formed, not lectured.

### Q4 — How are Elders removed?
- **Guardian:** Removal must be **possible, clean, and fast** (Art. X: no one above
  accountability). Triggers: any safeguarding flag → **immediate suspension, investigate
  after**; going dark / abandoning J-Shapers; turning witness into authority or ownership;
  seeking status/recognition (Gardener corruption).
- **Steward:** Removal is **protection of the field, not punitive drama.** Every Elder is
  accountable to an authority above them empowered to remove (launch: Drew; later: a steward
  layer). Seniority or relationships must **never** block removal.
- **Realist:** The hardest removals will be the *beloved, effective, slightly-too-central*
  Elder — the one accumulating a following. That is exactly the one the structure must be able
  to remove. Build the muscle before you need it.

### Q5 — How is witnessing protected from becoming authority?
- **Steward:** This is the constitutional crux (Witness-not-Confer). Protections must be
  **structural, not just cultural:**
  - Elders **cannot gate progress** — the system gives them no approve/deny button. They see
    and name; they never withhold. (Architecture enforces it, not goodwill.)
  - **Nothing for authority to accrue around:** no ranking of J-Shapers, no scores, no
    leaderboards.
  - **Circles, not thrones:** the group dilutes any one Elder's power; multiple witnesses.
  - **Accountable upward + removable** (Q4).
  - **Language discipline:** Elders "see and name," never "decide / pass / fail."
- **Steward (from Evidence doc):** the moment an Elder seeks the power to *decide* who
  advances, that desire itself disqualifies them.

### Q6 — How is authority prevented from becoming ownership?
- **Steward:** The deepest trap (Art. VIII; Journey Day 100). Protections:
  - **Frictionless exit** for every J-Shaper — leave any Elder/circle, no penalty, no shame.
    *The open door is the proof it was never ownership.*
  - **The one carried owes nothing** — no loyalty, gratitude, or staying required.
  - **No financial flow** from J-Shapers to Elders, ever. Status pays no money. (Kills the
    downline incentive at the root.)
  - **No captivity to one Elder** — a J-Shaper may request a different circle.
  - **The Father Test applied to Elders:** an Elder succeeds when the person no longer needs
    them. An Elder who *needs to be needed* is corrupting — trained against, and if persistent,
    removed.
  - **Transparency:** ownership behaviors (isolating a mentee, demanding loyalty, moving
    off-platform) are visible in logs and flagged.

## The Realist's closing — the three ways this breaks
1. **Scarcity throttles everything.** Accept it. Quality over scale. A tiny, real J-Shape
   beats a large, fake one. (J-1 + J-2 already choose this.)
2. **The charismatic Elder accumulates a following** — the cult risk. Guarded by circles,
   upward accountability, no-downline/no-money, removability, and no gating power.
3. **The founding few burn out.** Give-from-overflow (Day 97) applies to Elders too. Cap each
   Elder's load; protect the source; an exhausted Elder helps no one. Elder care is not
   optional — it is infrastructure.

## MVP of the Elder system (build/establish first)
- Drew + 2–4 vetted founding Elders (J-2). One circle. Get the DNA right.
- Capacity-gated intake (J-1) — entries throttled to what the circle can truly hold.
- Apprenticeship as the training method (Q3); written safeguarding protocol (Q4 triggers).
- Structural witness-not-gate: no approve/deny power in the tooling (Q5).
- Frictionless exit + no money flow wired in from day one (Q6).
- Adults only (J-3).
