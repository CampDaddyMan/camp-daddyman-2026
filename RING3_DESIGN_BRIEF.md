# RING 3 DESIGN BRIEF — Beginning to Give (Days 96–110)

> One-page brief governing the final Caterpillar arc. Written before further curriculum.
> Companion to `CATERPILLAR_FRAMEWORK_PROPOSAL.md` and `JOURNEY_CURRICULUM.md`.

## The single transformation Ring 3 is responsible for
**The shift from self-improvement to responsibility.**
Rings 1–2 made the user *capable*. Ring 3 turns that capability outward and makes the user
*responsible for someone other than themselves* — and then brings them to the edge of what
self-effort can do, so the J-Shape (surrender, a human Elder) becomes the only honest next
step. Beginning to give **is** the first acceptance of the trunk: *responsibility for the
future of others.*

---

## The seven questions

**1. What changes between Day 95 and Day 96?**
The axis flips. Days 1–95 the user was the *subject* being healed, fed, grown. From Day 96
the user becomes an *agent acting on the field.* The beneficiary moves from self → other.
The grammar moves from "I am becoming" to "I am responsible for."

**2. What becomes the primary currency?**
**Livity** — and only Livity. XP is gone here. The measure shifts from self-reported inner
work (reflections, challenges) to **community-witnessed service.** Evidence becomes
corroborated, never self-reported (per Evidence Architecture).

**3. How does Livity evolve from practice into identity?**
From *"I did a good thing today"* → *"I am someone who serves."* Repeated witnessed service
across the ring accumulates (identity follows action, Day 82) until, by Day 110, service is
not a task on a list — it is the user's default posture. Act → habit → identity.

**4. What behaviors prove readiness for Day 110?**
A *pattern* (not a score) of: service that was **witnessed**; service that **cost
something**; reaching **back** for someone behind; giving **without ownership/return**;
service to **outsiders**, not just one's own circle. And the decisive one: the user has hit
the **limit of self-effort** — discovered that everything they built is necessary but not
sufficient for what comes next. Readiness is *recognized, never tested.*

**5. What must be experienced before the J-Shape handoff?**
Three things, in reality not concept: (a) the **cost** of real giving; (b) giving **without
owning** the result; and — most important — (c) the **end of self-reliance.** The J-Shape is
surrender; you can only surrender what you've been gripping. Ring 3 must walk the user to
the wall of *"I cannot do the next part by trying harder,"* and toward *"I cannot do it
alone"* — so Day 110 hands them to a **human**, and they are ready to be met by one.

**6. What should never happen in Ring 3?**
- It must **never become "more self-improvement."** Center of gravity off the self.
- Livity must **never** be self-reported, gamified, scored, or leaderboarded.
- Giving must **never** curdle into ownership (guarded on Day 100).
- It must **not resolve** the J-Shape. Ring 3 *sets up* surrender; it does not deliver it.
- It must **not feel like graduation.** The Caterpillar ends on a holy *opening*, not a
  tidy closing. Pride/mastery is the enemy of the J-Shape.
- It must **not automate or fake** the human handoff. Day 110 points to a person.

**7. What is the user's emotional state on Day 110?**
Not triumphant. A **humbled fullness met by a holy restlessness:** *"I have become someone
who serves — and I have reached the end of what I can do by my own strength. I cannot do the
next part alone."* Surrender-ready. A little afraid (the J-Shape is where people quit), but
willing to hang there and be carried. Ready to be met by a human, not a screen.

## What the brief now governs for Days 101–110
The opening (96–100) covered the **ethics and reality of giving.** The back half must:
1. Deepen Livity from **act → identity** (a day naming "you are becoming a giver").
2. Widen service to the **outsider / the whole field** (canon: outsiders count equally).
3. Plant the **Become-the-Tending** seed (give the exact thing you most needed).
4. **Turn toward the limit of self** (101–110 must bend from "how to give" to "I cannot do
   the next part alone") so Day 110 is a true **surrender-handoff to a human**, not a victory
   lap. The Caterpillar must end *open.*
