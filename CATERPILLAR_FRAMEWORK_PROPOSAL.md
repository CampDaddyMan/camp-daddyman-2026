## The core shift from Egg → Caterpillar
- **Egg dismantled LIES.** It cleared what was false. Defensive. Subtractive.
- **Caterpillar builds CAPACITIES.** Now that the lies are cleared, what do you
  build in the empty ground? Constructive. Additive.

The caterpillar does one thing above all: **it eats.** It consumes constantly
to grow. So the spine of this phase is about *what you feed on* and *what you
begin to give back* — the first turn from pure consuming toward contributing.

## Proposed Daily DNA (parallel to the Egg's 8-part structure)
Instead of **The Lie → The Truth**, each Caterpillar day installs a practice:

1. **The Hunger** (what you've been feeding on — the old appetite)
2. **The Nourishment** (what to feed on instead — the practice to build)
3. **DaddyManism** (Drew's locked words)
4. **Reflection**
5. **Challenge** (an action that builds the capacity)
6. **Livity** (now weighted heavier than in the Egg — the give begins)
7. **Journal**
8. **Closing Seed**

## Proposed structure — 3 Rings (mirrors the Egg's 3-ring shape)

### RING 1 — CONSUMING WELL (building the inputs)
*"What am I feeding on?"* — Days 61–75 (proposed)
The caterpillar eats to grow. First it must learn to consume what builds rather
than what numbs. Discipline, attention, what you let into your mind and body.

Sample day themes (NOT written — for your approval):
- The appetite you inherited vs. the appetite you choose
- Attention as food — what you watch becomes what you are
- Discipline is not punishment; it is feeding yourself on purpose
- Rest as nourishment, not laziness
- The company you keep is something you consume

### RING 2 — GROWING VISIBLY (building the practices)
*"What am I becoming through repetition?"* — Days 76–95 (proposed)
The caterpillar's growth is visible — it sheds skin, gets larger. This ring is
about practices done in the open: habits, skills, showing up, the unglamorous
repetition that actually changes a person.

Sample day themes:
- Small reps beat big intentions
- Skill is earned in boredom, not inspiration
- Consistency is a form of self-respect
- Letting yourself be a beginner in public
- The molt — outgrowing a version of yourself on purpose

### RING 3 — BEGINNING TO GIVE (the first turn outward)
*"What can I give from what I've grown?"* — Days 96–110 (proposed)
The caterpillar starts as pure consumer and ends preparing to become something
that pollinates. This ring is the first deliberate turn from taking to giving —
where **Livity becomes the heavier currency** and XP starts to fade as the point.
This sets up the J-Shape (surrender), which is human-Elder territory, never automated.

Sample day themes:
- You have grown enough to give something now
- Giving from fullness vs. giving from depletion
- The first act of service that costs you something
- Mentoring down — reaching back for the next Egg
- Preparing for the J-Shape: what you cannot do alone
