# J-SHAPE PROMISE PROBLEM — Decision Memo

> **Purpose:** Define the unresolved promise created by Day 110, and lay out possible
> directions — **without choosing one.** This memo changes nothing. No doctrine change, no
> curriculum rewrite, no code, no Elder-expansion plan. It exists so Drew can rule.
> It modifies no frozen document; it only frames a question about them.

---

## 1. The problem, precisely

Day 110 ("The Hand in the Dark") makes a promise: at the threshold, *a real human reaches
for you* — *"we'll see you on the other side."* The wording implies the hand is **there.**

But the Ark has also accepted two things that make that hand **rare**:
- **Elder scarcity is a safeguard, not a bug** (we do not mass-produce Elders, lower the
  standard, or automate the human).
- **Decision J-1** throttles J-Shape entry to Elder capacity, so no one is abandoned at the
  worst moment.

Together these mean: **most people who reach Day 110 may find that the promised hand is not
yet available.** The dam holds (J-1) *and* the reservoir fills — a growing number who
finished the Caterpillar, are ready, and meet a hand that is, by design, scarce.

**This is not a growth problem. It is a promise problem.** The curriculum may be writing a
check the Elder supply is intentionally constrained from cashing.

---

## 2. The three truths any answer must preserve (non-negotiable)

1. **The hand must be real.** No automation, no bot, no canned stand-in for the human.
2. **The Elder must remain rare.** No mass-production, no lowered standard.
3. **The person must not be abandoned.** Especially not at the J-Shape — the place people quit.

The difficulty is that these three **pull against each other.** Keep the Elder rare → the
hand is often unavailable. Keep the promise of an immediate real hand → you must either
mass-produce Elders (violates #2) or fake it (violates #1). Refuse both → someone waits, and
the waiting risks feeling like abandonment (#3). **No direction satisfies all three for free.**

---

## 3. Where the tension actually lives

The promise is made by the **wording, pacing, and access logic** around Day 110 — not by the
Elder system itself. So the resolution likely lives there too: in *what is promised*, *when
the threshold is reached*, and *what "the hand" is allowed to be* — **not** in expanding the
Elder pool.

---

## 4. Possible directions (no recommendation — framing only)

**A. Reword the promise (honesty about the hand).**
Day 110 stops implying an immediate hand and instead promises a *real human when a real one
is available*, naming the wait as part of the hanging.
*Preserves:* all three, via honesty. *Costs:* less emotionally clean; the wait is named at
the exact quit-point; risk of discouragement.

**B. Re-pace access (enter the J-Shape only when a hand exists).**
Finishing the Caterpillar does not auto-trigger the J-Shape. The threshold is *approached*,
and *crossed* only when readiness **and** a real hand coincide. The throttle (J-1) moves to
the seam.
*Preserves:* #1, #2, and #3 (no locked door at the worst moment). *Costs:* the person is held
in a pre-J-Shape space; the "year" loosens; being held can feel like being held *back*.

**C. Redefine what "the hand" must be.**
Distinguish the **witness of surrender** (the rare Elder) from **companionship in the
waiting** (a circle of fellow J-Shapers — the "hanging together" the blueprint gestures at).
The Elder stays rare and reserved for the surrender; no one waits *alone*.
*Preserves:* #2 (Elder still rare), #1 (peer presence is real human contact), #3 (not alone).
*Costs:* risk of blurring witness vs. peer; safeguarding around peer contact; is peer-presence
*enough* at the hardest moment?

**D. Reframe the wait as sacred (meaning, not mechanism).**
Accept that the J-Shape *is* a season of hanging, and waiting for a hand is part of the
surrender, not a failure of it. The person is honestly companioned (app as container, honest
status) but the rare Elder arrives when they arrive.
*Preserves:* all three by redefining "abandoned" (honest companionship ≠ abandonment).
*Costs:* a thin line between *sacred waiting* and *abandonment* — and the person at the
quit-point may not feel the difference.

**E. Disclosed-conditional promise (radical honesty at the seam).**
Before the threshold, tell the truth plainly: *"The next part requires a real human. Real
ones are rare and worth waiting for. We will not fake it, and we will not abandon you."* The
promise becomes explicitly conditional rather than implicitly immediate.
*Preserves:* all three, via disclosure. *Costs:* less magical; sets an expectation of delay
up front, which may deter some before they arrive.

*(These are not mutually exclusive — a ruling may combine, e.g., E's honesty + C's peer
companionship + B's pacing.)*

---

## 5. What this memo is NOT
- Not a doctrine change. Not a curriculum rewrite. Not code. Not an Elder-expansion plan.
- Not a choice. No direction is endorsed here.
- It touches no frozen document — it only names the question.

---

## 6. The decision for Drew

**Question to rule on:** *When a person reaches the J-Shape and no Elder is available, what
does the Ark do — such that the hand stays real, the Elder stays rare, and the person is not
abandoned?*

Pick a direction, combine several, or reject all and name a sixth.

**What reality has not yet voted on (open):**
- Is *companionship by peers* enough at the threshold, or must the first hand be an Elder's?
- Does honest *waiting* read as sacred or as abandonment to the person actually in it?
- Should Day 110's wording be made conditional **now**, or only once the J-Shape goes live?
- Is the reservoir (people held before the J-Shape) itself a place that needs designing?

Until Drew rules, Day 110 stands as written, and the promise remains — knowingly — open.
